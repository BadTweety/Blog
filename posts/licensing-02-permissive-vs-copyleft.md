The single most useful distinction in open-source licensing.

## Permissive (MIT, Apache-2.0, BSD)

Do almost anything — use, modify, sell — as long as you keep the copyright notice. Apache-2.0 adds an explicit patent grant.

## Copyleft (GPL, AGPL)

You may use and modify the code, but **derivative works must carry the same license**. That's the "viral" part people worry about.

| License | Commercial use | Must open-source your changes |
|---|---|---|
| MIT | yes | no |
| Apache-2.0 | yes | no |
| GPL-3.0 | yes | yes, if you distribute |
| AGPL-3.0 | yes | yes, even over a network |
